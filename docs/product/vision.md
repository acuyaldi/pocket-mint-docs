# Pocket Mint Vision

## Mission

Give people a private, trustworthy understanding of their financial life so they can make confident decisions while remaining in control of their data.

---

## Vision Statement

Pocket Mint envisions personal finance without the usual trade-off between clarity and privacy. People should be able to understand what they own, what they owe, how their position is changing, and why every number appears—without surrendering control of their financial information.

Pocket Mint will become a durable financial companion for people who value independence: one calm place to understand everyday money, debt, installments, and progress over time, supported by transparent automation that remains accountable to the user.

---

## Product Identity

Pocket Mint is a privacy-first personal finance workspace for understanding and managing a financial position across tracked assets, debt, transactions, and installments. It turns fragmented financial activity into an organized, explainable view that helps users act with confidence.

Pocket Mint is not a financial institution, financial adviser, accounting suite, trading product, or social network. It does not move money on the user's behalf, prescribe financial choices, or obscure financial reality behind opaque scores and unexplained recommendations.

---

## Target Users

### Primary Users

Pocket Mint is primarily for privacy-conscious individuals who actively manage multiple wallets, Credit Cards, PayLater, or Installments and want a trustworthy view of their financial position. They value control, accurate debt visibility, and clear explanations more than novelty or excessive customization.

### Secondary Users

Pocket Mint also serves people building stronger financial habits, users whose financial lives are becoming more complex, and people who want automation to reduce repetitive bookkeeping without surrendering judgment or control.

### Who Pocket Mint Is Not Built For

Pocket Mint is not built for professional accountants, active traders, businesses requiring formal accounting, people seeking a managed banking service, or users who want public, competitive, or social financial experiences.

---

## Product Values

**Privacy First**

Financial information is deeply personal. Privacy exists to protect the user's autonomy, dignity, and confidence in recording an honest financial picture.

**Financial Clarity**

Pocket Mint exists to make financial position and movement understandable. A feature has value only when it reduces uncertainty or helps the user make a better-informed decision.

**Deterministic Calculations**

The same facts must always produce the same result. Determinism exists so users can trust comparisons, reproduce outcomes, and distinguish truth from interpretation.

**Explainable Numbers**

Every financial figure must have a clear meaning, source, and derivation. Explainability exists because unexplained numbers weaken trust and can lead to harmful decisions.

**Ownership**

The user remains the authority over their financial information, access, corrections, and consequential actions. Ownership exists to ensure the product serves the user rather than controlling them.

**Transparency**

Pocket Mint communicates what is known, what is incomplete, and what is projected. Transparency exists to prevent estimates, gaps, and assumptions from being mistaken for financial facts.

**Automation with User Control**

Automation should remove repetitive effort while remaining visible, reversible where appropriate, and subordinate to user intent. It exists to assist judgment, never replace it.

---

## Design Philosophy

Pocket Mint should feel calm, trustworthy, professional, data-first, focused, and organized. The experience should give users a sense of controlled transparency: complex finances become understandable without feeling simplified beyond recognition.

The product should create confidence through clear hierarchy, predictable behavior, honest states, and purposeful restraint. It should feel serious enough for important financial decisions and approachable enough for everyday use.

---

## UX Principles

1. Never surprise the user with a financial consequence.
2. Every number must be explainable.
3. Present the most important financial information first.
4. Prefer fast, focused workflows over flashy interactions.
5. Prefer consistency over cleverness.
6. Distinguish recorded facts, derived metrics, and projections clearly.
7. Require user intent for consequential or destructive actions.
8. Preserve context so users can understand what changed and why.
9. Reveal complexity progressively without hiding essential information.
10. Show gaps honestly; never invent certainty or financial history.

---

## Product Principles

1. Every feature must increase financial clarity, user control, or both.
2. Privacy is a default product boundary, not an optional enhancement.
3. Every financial concept has one canonical meaning and one authoritative rule.
4. Every financial calculation must be deterministic and reproducible.
5. Every displayed financial fact must trace back to recorded information.
6. Automation must never reduce the user's ability to understand, confirm, correct, or stop an action.
7. Approved product documentation is the source of truth for product behavior and language.
8. Product Decisions resolve genuine ambiguity before dependent work proceeds.
9. Historical truth must be preserved; corrections must remain explainable.
10. Scope discipline is a feature: Pocket Mint should deepen its core purpose before broadening it.

---

## Long-term Direction

Within a three-to-five-year horizon, Pocket Mint should be a trusted personal financial command center for users who want independence from opaque financial platforms. It should provide a coherent view of current position, debt, cash flow, obligations, and progress over time, while making the origin and meaning of every figure clear.

Pocket Mint should grow toward richer historical understanding, proactive but explainable insights, and user-governed automation across daily financial routines. Growth should deepen financial clarity and reduce bookkeeping effort without turning Pocket Mint into a financial institution, adviser, or general-purpose accounting product.

---

## Non-goals

Pocket Mint will intentionally never become:

- An online bank or payment provider.
- Accounting software for businesses or regulated reporting.
- A stock, investment, or cryptocurrency trading platform.
- A budgeting-envelope system: zero-based allocation of unspent funds across categories, with rollover between them. (Category-level monthly spend-limit tracking against recorded transactions — Budgeting, [PD-009](./decisions/009-budgeting-scope.md), Approved — is a distinct, narrower feature: it never allocates, moves, or rolls over funds between categories.)
- A lending or credit-underwriting service.
- A financial adviser that prescribes decisions.
- A social, competitive, or public finance application.
- A marketplace for financial products.
- A collaborative workspace for shared organizational finances.
- A product that monetizes access to the user's financial information.
